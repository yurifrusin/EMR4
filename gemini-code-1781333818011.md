# EMR4 Centaur: Clinical Data Pipeline & AI Fine-Tuning Strategy

This document outlines the architecture and regulatory strategy for ingesting, de-identifying, and leveraging a historical dataset of ~30,000 structured Word documents (representing ~750,000 clinical encounters). This data will serve as the foundational "memory" and training corpus for the Centaur AI engine.

## 1. The Value Proposition (The "Centaur Brain")

The historical dataset provides localized, domain-specific Australian general practice data, moving the AI from "generic textbook" to "specialized clinic."

* **Semantic Search & Few-Shot RAG:** By embedding these encounters into the PostgreSQL `pgvector` database, the DDx Assistant can perform similarity searches against real historical cases to ground its diagnostic reasoning.
* **The Billing Oracle (Fine-Tuning):** Mapping historical clinical notes to their finalized MBS/SNOMED codes allows us to fine-tune a model specifically for Australian clinical coding, teaching it the nuanced thresholds between different item numbers (e.g., Item 23 vs. Item 36).
* **Style & Tone Alignment:** Fine-tuning on actual GP notes teaches the AI to adopt localized medical shorthand, structure, and brevity.

## 2. Data Curation: The "Golden Dataset" Strategy

To avoid the "garbage in, garbage out" trap, the data will not be treated equally. Because the historical authors (GPs) are known, the dataset will be manually graded and segmented prior to ingestion:

* **Tier 1 (The "Golden Dataset"):** Encounters authored by the most trusted, meticulous GPs. This tier will be used exclusively for **Fine-Tuning** to teach the AI *how* to write and code perfectly.
* **Tier 2 & 3 (The General Corpus):** Rushed or inconsistently coded notes. This tier will *not* be used for fine-tuning behaviors, but will be embedded for **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** to retain valuable historical context regarding local community health trends, disease presentations, and vernacular.

## 3. The De-Identification Architecture (PHI Leakage Prevention)

Stripping standard demographic headers (Name, DOB, Medicare) is insufficient for clinical AI, as "PHI Leakage" frequently occurs within the free-text clinical narrative (e.g., references to specific occupations, family members, or exact dates of injury). 

To guarantee regulatory compliance and protect patient privacy, all free-text must pass through an automated NLP scrubbing engine before touching the vector database or training pipeline.

* **Core Technology:** Integration of **Microsoft Presidio** (an open-source data protection and anonymization SDK) into the ingestion script.
* **Functionality:** Presidio will actively scan the narrative text and replace identifiable entities (names, locations, phone numbers, exact dates) with generic tags (e.g., `<PERSON>`, `<LOCATION>`, `<DATE>`).

## 4. The Data Extraction Pipeline

A dedicated Python utility will be built to process the 30k Word documents and transform them into AI-ready `JSONL` format. 

**Pipeline Steps:**
1.  **The Parser (`python-docx`):** Iterates through the local folder of Word documents.
2.  **The Slicer:** Programmatically strips out the "Demographics" header completely. Extracts only the text residing under the "Clinical Notes" and "Imported Results" headings.
3.  **The Scrubber (Presidio):** Passes the extracted clinical narrative through Microsoft Presidio to redact all embedded PHI and PII.
4.  **The Formatter:** Packages the sanitized text, along with the extracted MBS/SNOMED codes and the GP Quality Tier tag, into a `JSONL` object.
